# Customer Churn Analysis for Telecom Industry

## Project Overview

Customer churn is a major challenge for telecom companies because losing customers directly affects revenue and business growth.

This project analyzes telecom customer data to identify patterns, trends, and key factors that influence customer churn. Using Python and data visualization techniques, the analysis uncovers insights related to customer contracts, payment methods, tenure, demographics, and internet services.

The goal of this project is to help telecom companies understand churn behavior and develop effective customer retention strategies.

## Business Problem

Telecom companies experience significant revenue loss when customers discontinue their services. Understanding why customers leave is essential for improving customer satisfaction and retention.

This project answers key questions such as:

- Which customers are most likely to churn?

- How does contract type affect churn?

- Does payment method influence customer  retention?

- How does customer tenure impact churn behavior?

## Key Insights
### Contract Type

Customers with **month-to-month contracts** have the highest churn rate (around 42%), while customers with long-term contracts show significantly lower churn.

### Payment Method

Customers using **electronic checks** show the highest churn rate (approximately 45%), while customers using credit cards or bank transfers churn less frequently.

### Customer Tenure

Customers with **less than one year of tenure** have the highest probability of churn, with rates around 50%. Churn decreases significantly as tenure increases.

### Internet Service Type

Customers using fiber optic internet services exhibit higher churn compared to DSL users.

### Senior Citizens

Senior customers show a higher churn rate compared to non-senior customers, indicating a need for targeted customer retention strategies.

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following strategies can help reduce churn:

- Promote **long-term contracts** to increase customer retention

- Encourage customers to switch to **stable payment methods**

- Improve **customer engagement during the first year**

- Provide **special support programs for senior customers**

- Investigate service quality issues for **fiber optic users**

These actions can significantly improve customer retention and customer satisfaction.
